ABOUT ME

I have always been fascinated with the sciences, specifically with understanding how systems work. From the intricacies of our planet's ecosystems to the inner workings of our bodies, I've been captivated by the complexity of it all. After graduating from high school, I discovered a new interest: computers. During that summer, I worked at a restaurant bussing tables and used my earnings to build my first PC. It was a tedious yet fulfilling experience, as I researched and installed each component. This sparked my curiosity in programming and paved the way for my future career.

I considered computers a hobby while pursuing a major in music education. However, after taking a free programming course online, I discovered my passion for it and switched my career goal to becoming a software architect. Programmers play a crucial role in driving technological progress, and I am eager to create sophisticated, groundbreaking systems that enhance people's lives worldwide.

As a self-funded student throughout my time in college, I had to balance my studies with work, which ultimately led to a longer-than-average undergraduate journey. Despite the challenges, I maintained an exceptional academic record, achieving a 3.9 GPA on an accelerated course load while working the maximum hours as a student employee at NEIU.
